GraphID

Summary

GraphID, conceptualized and developed during the ETHParis Hackathon, is an innovative tool designed to enhance social visibility across both Web2 and Web3 platforms. The platform allows users to create and explore social graphs, connecting various online identities and projects, thereby offering a comprehensive view of one's digital social landscape.

Approach

The design of GraphID was a thoughtful process of aesthetic appeal and functional clarity. The dark mode interface, combined with the vibrant web3 color palette, creates a visually engaging experience, while the user-friendly features like natural language search and a spacebar-activated input makes the platform accessible and easy to navigate.

Embracing the popular trend in user interface design, GraphID was developed with a dark mode theme. This choice not only aligns with the preferences of many tech-savvy users but also provides a sleek and modern backdrop that highlights other design elements, particularly the vibrant neon accents.
Social Graph
Recognizing the complexity of social graphs, especially when spanning multiple platforms, the design focuses on simplifying the visualization and navigation of these networks. The interface is crafted to present complex data in an intuitive and digestible format.
